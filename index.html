My Story

I took the scenic route towards data science.

I got cold feet before starting my PhD in Neuromechanics.

Data processing has a breadth of applications, so why was there so little room for interdisciplinary collaboration on the path that I was on? My dream was to create solutions without being pigeon-holed into one specialization for the rest of my life.

A cautious career pivot, I first deferred my PhD offer to work as a research assistant in the related field of healthcare. The experience of communicating research insights to patients and healthcare professionals was indispensable.

Still, something was missing for me...

I wanted the skills to wrangle, analyze and visualize large amounts of data. I wanted to learn to write code.

Fast forward to me diving into my career transformation by completing my Diploma in Data Science. I now have a near-obsession with coding. It's an exhilarating thing, being able to refine big datasets and design visualizations by writing lines of code.

I can't wait to use my skills to support a team in activating their data insights.

This career pivot hasn't been easy. And although it wasn't the traditional route to data science, I am grateful to all of my past opportunities. Excuse my cheesiness as I quote Drizzy a.k.a. Drake quoting optimist Ralph Waldo Emerson:

"Sometimes it's the journey that teaches you a lot about your destination."
