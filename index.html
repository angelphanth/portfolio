THE CODE

❮ ❯

I pivoted from a PhD in Neuromechanics.

A large contributor to my cold feet: Having my first taste of coding during data analysis for my MSc.

A cautious career pivot, I first deferred my PhD offer to work with data, but in clinical settings. Turns out that patients experiencing high-risk pregnancies (Sinai Health Systems) or requiring gum tissue grafts (Dr. Peter C. Fritz Dentistry Professional Corporation) have more pertinent interests than deciphering wordy study findings.

The takeaway: Insights, no matter how impactful, won't get activated if the people that would benefit can't make sense of them.

Fast forward to me taking the plunge into my career change and completing my Diploma in Data Science. I take what I learned working in healthcare into everything I do. As a data science teaching assistant, I geared my code-alongs and student hours to the intended audience's needs. I am eager to support a team in communicating data effectively to activate insights and help others.
